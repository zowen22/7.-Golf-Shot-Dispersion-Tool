# Problem Statement

-----

## Problem

Most strokes lost by amateur golfers aren't from poor mechanics — they're from poor course management. Golfers have no tool that shows realistic shot outcome zones on an actual hole map. Yardage apps tell you distance; no app tells you where you'll realistically end up given your skill level and club selection.

## Target User

Amateur golfers (handicap 5–30+) who want to lower their score through smarter decisions, not swing changes. Use case splits into:
- **On the course** — real-time shot planning during a round
- **Anywhere** — studying a course before playing it

## Goals

- Deliver caddie-level strategic insight via an interactive dispersion-based hole map
- Show realistic shot outcome zones (not just yardages) based on the user's actual skill level
- Progressive personalization: works from day one without club data, gets better as user adds bag details
- Subscription revenue model targeting serious amateur golfers

## Core Features

1. **Dispersion ellipse on satellite hole map** — drag-to-aim, live updates
2. **Tiered dispersion system** — formula-based (day 1) → club distances → per-club dispersions → GPS history (post-MVP)
3. **Two entry modes** — Start a Round (GPS auto-detect) and Study Course (manual search)
4. **Bag management** — club distances and optional dispersion widths unlock progressive features
5. **Onboarding flow** — 12-screen sequence collecting minimum data needed for day-1 personalization
6. **Subscription paywall** — weekly ($15) and quarterly ($8.33/mo) tiers via StoreKit 2
7. **Auth** — Sign in with Apple (required), Google, and email/password via Firebase

## Constraints

- iOS only (SwiftUI, iOS 16 minimum deployment)
- Solo/small team build — framework choices optimize for speed and maintainability
- No Xcode access in remote build environment — Swift source scaffold must be Xcode-ready but compiled there
- MVP scope: dispersion map, onboarding, auth, bag management, course search — scorecard and GPS shot tracking are post-MVP
